## Overview

**The Infiltrators & The Ghost** is a social deduction game where players use wordplay, observation, and tactical voting to achieve their team's victory. The game is played by passing a single device around, making it perfect for parties and gatherings.

## Setup

### Players
- **Minimum**: 3 players
- **Maximum**: 10 players
- **Recommended**: 5-7 players for optimal gameplay

### Roles Distribution
The game automatically assigns:
- **1 Undercover Agent**
- **1 Mr. White**
- **Remaining players**: Civilians

Example with 6 players:
- 4 Civilians
- 1 Undercover
- 1 Mr. White

## Game Flow

### Phase 1: Role Assignment (Secret)
1. The app randomly assigns roles
2. Players pass the device around
3. Each player privately views their role and word
4. **Critical**: Keep your role and word secret!

### Phase 2: Description Round
1. Players sit in a circle or agreed order
2. Each player gives **ONE clue** about their word
3. Clues should be:
   - Single words or short phrases
   - Verbal (spoken aloud)
   - Related to your word (or improvised if you're Mr. White!)

**Example Clues**:
- Civilian (‡§ï‡§ø‡§§‡§æ‡§¨): "Pages", "Reading", "Library"
- Undercover (Novel): "Story", "Author", "Fiction"
- Mr. White: "Information", "Paper" (guessing based on others)

### Phase 3: Discussion
1. After all players give clues, open discussion begins
2. Players debate who seems suspicious
3. Look for:
   - Inconsistent clues
   - Overly vague descriptions
   - Players who seem confused
   - Clues that don't match the pattern

**Tips**:
- Ask questions
- Challenge suspicious players
- Build alliances (but be careful!)
- Infiltrators might try to frame Civilians

### Phase 4: Voting
1. Group votes to eliminate one player
2. Tap on a player's avatar to vote
3. Player with most votes is eliminated
4. Eliminated player's role is **revealed immediately**

**Voting Strategy**:
- Civilians: Try to eliminate infiltrators
- Infiltrators: Try to eliminate Civilians OR frame each other (risky!)
- Consider voting patterns and alliances

### Phase 5: Role Reveal
- The eliminated player's role and word are shown to everyone
- This is a critical information moment!
- Adjust your strategy based on who was eliminated

### Phase 6: Continue or Victory
The game checks for victory conditions:

**If no one wins**: Return to Phase 2 (new round)

**If victory condition met**: Game ends!

## Advanced Strategy

### For Civilians
1. **Don't be too obvious early** - Undercover might figure out the word
2. **Build trust networks** - Find other Civilians
3. **Look for outliers** - Who's giving different types of clues?
4. **Count votes** - Who's voting suspiciously?
5. **Remember past clues** - Patterns emerge over rounds

### For Undercover
1. **Listen first** - Let Civilians give clues before you
2. **Stay in the middle** - Not too specific, not too vague
3. **Find Mr. White subtly** - You need each other!
4. **Redirect suspicion** - Point to the most vocal Civilian
5. **Know when to sacrifice** - Sometimes throwing Mr. White under the bus helps you survive

### For Mr. White
1. **Ultra-generic clues** - "Common", "Useful", "Important"
2. **Echo patterns** - Use similar adjectives to others
3. **Confident delivery** - Act like you know exactly what you're talking about
4. **Identify word category quickly** - Object? Concept? Activity?
5. **Partner with Undercover** - Once identified, work together

## Special Scenarios

### Tie Votes
If voting results in a tie:
- Discuss and vote again
- OR skip elimination for that round

### Player Dropout
If a player must leave:
- That player is eliminated
- Role is revealed
- Game continues

### Accidental Reveal
If a player accidentally reveals their word:
- Group decides if they should be eliminated
- Or continue with handicap

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can Mr. White win alone?**
A: No, at least one infiltrator must survive to win.

**Q: What if everyone figures out both words immediately?**
A: Focus on behavior and voting patterns. The infiltrators still need to survive!

**Q: Can I change my clue after giving it?**
A: No, clues are final once spoken.

**Q: What if I accidentally see someone else's screen?**
A: Be honest and inform the group. They decide how to proceed.

**Q: How long does a game typically last?**
A: 10-20 minutes depending on player count and discussion length.

**Q: Can we play with kids?**
A: Yes! Use Easy mode. Recommended for ages 10+.

**Q: What if the Undercover's word is too obvious?**
A: That's part of the challenge! Skill matters more than luck.

**Q: Can Civilians accidentally eliminate each other?**
A: Yes! That's why discussion and careful observation are important.
